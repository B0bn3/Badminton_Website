Basics

What is Badminton?

Badminton is a fast-paced racket sport played by two players (singles) or four players (doubles). The aim is to hit a shuttlecock over a net so that it lands in your opponent’s court. It is known for its quick reactions, fast rallies, and exciting smashes. Badminton can be played indoors or outdoors, but professional games are always played indoors to avoid wind interference.

Rules and Scoring

A match is best of three games. Each game is played to 21 points. You score a point whenever you win a rally, no matter who served. If the score reaches 20–20, a player must win by two points (e.g., 22–20, 23–21). Players change ends after each game and when the leading score reaches 11 points in the deciding game.

Different Types of Shots

Smash – A powerful downward shot aimed to end the rally quickly. Clear – A high, deep shot to push your opponent to the back of the court. Drop Shot – A gentle shot that just clears the net, forcing your opponent to run forward. Net Shot – A soft, controlled shot played close to the net, often used to set up a winning smash.
